## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes [Organisation Name]'s commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. It sets the overall direction and principles for information security management across the organisation and serves as the foundation for the Information Security Management System (ISMS) established in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties with access to [Organisation Name]'s information systems or data
- All information assets owned, controlled, or processed by [Organisation Name]
- All locations and environments where [Organisation Name]'s information is stored, processed, or transmitted
- All technology systems, networks, applications, and devices used to process organisational information

**ISMS Scope boundary:** [Reference ISMS-SCOPE-001 for full scope statement]

---

## 3. Information Security Objectives

[Organisation Name] is committed to achieving and maintaining the following information security objectives, which are reviewed annually:

1. **Confidentiality:** Ensure that information is accessible only to those authorised to access it
2. **Integrity:** Safeguard the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods
3. **Availability:** Ensure that authorised users have access to information and associated assets when required
4. **Compliance:** Meet all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations relating to information security
5. **Continual Improvement:** Continuously improve the effectiveness of the ISMS

Measurable IS Objectives are maintained separately in [ISMS-OBJ-001].

---

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Risk Management
[Organisation Name] shall identify, assess, and treat information security risks in accordance with the organisation's risk appetite. All significant IS risks shall be documented, owned, and subject to treatment plans.

### 4.2 Legal and Regulatory Compliance
[Organisation Name] shall identify and comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements related to information security, including but not limited to UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and sector-specific regulations.

### 4.3 Asset Management
All information assets shall be inventoried, classified, and protected in accordance with their classification level and the risks associated with them.

### 4.4 Access Control
Access to information and information systems shall be granted on the basis of business need and least privilege. All access shall be authorised, reviewed, and revoked when no longer required.

### 4.5 Human Resources Security
All staff shall receive appropriate information security awareness training. Background screening shall be conducted for roles with access to sensitive information. IS responsibilities shall be included in employment contracts.

### 4.6 Physical and Environmental Security
Facilities containing information processing equipment shall be protected by appropriate physical security controls to prevent unauthorised access, damage, or interference.

### 4.7 Supplier Security
Third-party suppliers with access to [Organisation Name]'s information or systems shall be subject to IS assessment and contractual requirements. Their IS performance shall be monitored.

### 4.8 Incident Management
All information security events and incidents shall be reported, logged, and responded to in accordance with the IS Incident Response Procedure. Incidents shall be analysed to prevent recurrence.

### 4.9 Business Continuity
[Organisation Name] shall maintain plans to ensure the continuity of critical information processing activities and recovery from IS disruptions within agreed recovery objectives.

### 4.10 Cryptography
Cryptographic controls shall be used to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of information in accordance with the Cryptography Policy.

---

## 6. Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy and supporting IS procedures may result in:
- Disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal
- Legal action in cases of deliberate breach or gross negligence
- Termination of contractor or supplier agreements
- Regulatory investigation and penalties

---

## 7. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually, or following:
- A significant change to the organisation's structure, operations, or IT environment
- A significant IS incident
- Changes in applicable laws or regulations
- Recommendations from internal audit or management review
