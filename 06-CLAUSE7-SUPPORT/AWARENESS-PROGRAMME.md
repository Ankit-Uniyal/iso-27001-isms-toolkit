# Information Security Awareness Programme

**Document ID:** ISMS-AWARE-001
**Version:** 1.0 | **Owner:** CISO / HR | **Date:** ___ | **Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## Purpose

This document defines [Organisation Name]'s IS awareness, education, and training programme in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 7.3 and Annex A control 6.3. All staff must understand the IS Policy and their obligations, the contribution they make to ISMS effectiveness, and the implications of not conforming to IS requirements.

---

## Programme Objectives

1. Ensure all staff understand the IS Policy and their role in protecting information
2. Ensure all staff are aware of their IS obligations and consequences of non-compliance
3. Build a security-aware culture across the organisation
4. Reduce human-factor IS incidents (phishing, social engineering, accidental disclosure)
5. Meet legal requirements (UK GDPR staff training obligations)

---

## Target Audiences

| Audience | IS Training Requirement | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| All staff | IS Policy awareness; acceptable use; phishing; incident reporting; clear desk | Annual mandatory |
| New starters | IS induction covering all baseline topics | Within first 2 weeks |
| IT / Security staff | Advanced IS topics; role-specific technical training | Annual + ongoing CPD |
| Management / Senior leadership | IS risk governance; ISMS obligations | Annual |
| Third parties / contractors | Supplier IS requirements; acceptable use | At onboarding; annually if long-term |
| Developers | Secure coding; OWASP top 10; SDLC security | Annual |
| Finance / HR staff | Data handling; phishing; social engineering; fraud awareness | Annual; after incidents |
