## Statement of Commitment

I, the Chief Executive Officer of Nexus Financial Services Ltd, on behalf of the Board of Directors and Senior Leadership Team, hereby confirm our unconditional commitment to the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS) in accordance with **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**.

Nexus Financial Services Ltd operates as an FCA-regulated financial services provider handling sensitive customer financial data, personally identifiable information (PII), and proprietary business assets. We recognise that information security is fundamental to maintaining customer trust, satisfying regulatory obligations, and achieving our strategic objectives.

---

## Our Commitments

### 1. Strategic Alignment
Information security objectives are aligned with the organisation's strategic direction. The ISMS is embedded within NFS's corporate governance framework and is reviewed as part of the annual business planning cycle.

### 2. Policy and Framework
We have established, documented, and communicated an Information Security Policy (NFS-IS-POL-001) that reflects the organisation's commitment to protecting information assets. All policies are reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in the threat landscape, regulatory environment, and business operations.

### 3. Resources and Investment
The Board commits to providing adequate resources — financial, human, and technological — to implement and sustain the ISMS. This includes:
- Dedicated ISMS Manager and Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) roles
- Budget allocation for information security tools, training, and certification
- Access to external expertise and certification bodies as required

### 4. Integration with Business Processes
Information security requirements are integrated into all business processes, projects, and change management activities at NFS. Security by design is a mandatory consideration in new product development, system implementation, and supplier engagement.

### 5. Regulatory Compliance
NFS is committed to meeting all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements related to information security, including but not limited to:
- UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
- Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) SYSC requirements
- Network and Information Systems (NIS) Regulations
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)

### 6. Risk-Based Approach
We adopt a risk-based approach to information security, ensuring that controls are proportionate to identified risks. Risk assessments are conducted regularly, and the risk treatment plan is reviewed and updated to address emerging threats.

### 7. Awareness and Competence
All personnel with information security responsibilities are competent and receive appropriate training. A mandatory information security awareness programme ensures all staff understand their obligations and the importance of protecting NFS's information assets.

### 8. Continual Improvement
The ISMS is subject to regular internal audits, management reviews, and performance monitoring. Nonconformities are addressed through a formal corrective action process, and opportunities for improvement are actively sought and implemented.

### 9. Leadership Visibility
Senior management actively demonstrates leadership in information security by:
- Participating in management reviews of the ISMS
- Championing a security-first culture across the organisation
- Holding business unit leaders accountable for information security performance within their areas
