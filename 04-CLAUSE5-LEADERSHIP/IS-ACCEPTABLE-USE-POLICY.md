## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines the standards for acceptable and unacceptable use of information assets, information systems, and technology resources owned, leased, or operated by Nexus Financial Services Ltd (NFS). It supports the organisation's commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All NFS employees (permanent, fixed-term, part-time)
- Contractors, consultants, and agency workers
- Third-party suppliers and partners with access to NFS systems
- All NFS information assets, systems, networks, and devices
- All locations including NFS offices, remote working locations, and cloud environments

---

## 2. Information Asset Classification

All information assets must be handled in accordance with their classification level:

| Classification | Description | Handling Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Information approved for public release | No restrictions on distribution |
| **Internal** | General business information | Internal distribution only |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business and customer data | Need-to-know basis; encryption required in transit |
| **Highly Restricted** | Regulated data, strategic plans, PII | Strict access controls; encryption at rest and in transit |

All NFS employees are responsible for classifying information they create and handling it according to its classification.

---

## 3. Acceptable Use — Information Systems

### 3.1 General System Use
- Use NFS systems and resources primarily for legitimate business purposes
- Access only systems, applications, and data required for your role
- Log off or lock workstations when leaving them unattended
- Use only NFS-approved software and applications on corporate devices
- Keep system access credentials confidential and never share them

### 3.2 Email and Communications
- Use NFS email for professional communication; personal use must be minimal and not disruptive
- Do not send Confidential or Highly Restricted information via unencrypted email
- Be vigilant about phishing; verify unexpected requests before clicking links or opening attachments
- Do not use personal email accounts for NFS business communications

### 3.3 Internet and Web Browsing
- Internet access is provided for business purposes; incidental personal use is permitted
- Do not access websites that are illegal, offensive, discriminatory, or reputationally damaging to NFS
- Do not download software, applications, or files from untrusted or unauthorised sources
- Do not use personal cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud) for NFS business data

### 3.4 Mobile Devices and Remote Working
- All mobile devices used to access NFS systems must be enrolled in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) system
- Enable screen lock with a minimum 6-digit PIN or biometric authentication
- Report lost or stolen devices to the IT Service Desk immediately (within 1 hour)
- Connect to NFS systems remotely only via the approved VPN solution
- Do not access NFS systems from public Wi-Fi without using the VPN

### 3.5 Removable Media
- Use of removable media (USB drives, external hard drives) requires prior approval from the CISO
- All NFS-approved removable media must be encrypted
- Do not connect personal removable media to NFS equipment
- Do not copy Highly Restricted data to removable media without written authorisation

---

## 4. Acceptable Use — Data and Information

### 4.1 Customer Data
- Access customer data only when required to perform your job function
- Do not access customer accounts for personal curiosity or to assist friends and family
- Do not copy, export, or retain customer data beyond what is necessary for the task
- Report any suspected unauthorised access to customer data immediately

### 4.2 Personal Data (UK GDPR Compliance)
- Handle personal data in accordance with NFS's Data Protection Policy and UK GDPR obligations
- Do not process personal data for purposes other than those for which it was collected
- Ensure personal data is accurate, kept up to date, and not retained longer than necessary
- Do not transmit personal data outside the UK/EEA without appropriate safeguards

### 4.3 Intellectual Property
- Do not copy, reproduce, or distribute copyrighted materials without appropriate licensing
- NFS-developed software, processes, and documentation are the intellectual property of NFS
- Do not disclose proprietary NFS information to competitors, media, or unauthorised third parties

---

## 5. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on NFS systems and networks:

**Security Violations:**
- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to any system, network, or data
- Bypassing, disabling, or circumventing security controls
- Installing or executing malware, hacking tools, or unauthorised software
- Conducting port scans, vulnerability assessments, or penetration tests without written authorisation
- Intercepting network traffic (packet sniffing) without authorisation

**Data Misuse:**
- Accessing, copying, or exfiltrating data beyond your authorised scope
- Sharing passwords, access tokens, or authentication credentials
- Removing or destroying information assets without following the data disposal procedure
- Using NFS data for personal gain or competitive advantage

**System Misuse:**
- Using NFS systems for cryptocurrency mining
- Running personal business activities using NFS resources
- Storing, transmitting, or accessing illegal content (child sexual abuse material, extremist content)
- Engaging in harassment, discrimination, or bullying via NFS systems

**Communications Misuse:**
- Sending unsolicited bulk email (spam) from NFS systems
- Misrepresenting your identity or impersonating colleagues
- Disclosing NFS confidential information to unauthorised parties

---

## 6. Social Media

- Do not disclose NFS confidential information, customer data, or business strategy on social media
- Personal social media use during working hours should be minimal and non-disruptive
- Do not make statements on behalf of NFS without authorisation from Marketing/Communications
- Clearly identify personal opinions as your own and not those of NFS
- Do not post photographs or videos of NFS premises, systems, or customers without permission

---

## 7. Monitoring and Audit

NFS reserves the right to monitor, log, and audit all activity on its information systems to:
- Ensure compliance with this policy
- Investigate suspected security incidents
- Comply with legal and regulatory requirements

Monitoring is conducted in accordance with UK GDPR and relevant employment law. Staff will be made aware of monitoring through this policy and the employee handbook.

---

## 8. Reporting Violations and Security Incidents

All staff must report:
- Suspected policy violations
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspected phishing or social engineering attempts
- Observed unauthorised system access

**Report to:** IT Service Desk — itsecurity@nexusfs.co.uk | 0161 700 0001 (internal ext. 4000)
**Out of Hours:** CISO on-call: +44 7700 900 001

---

## 9. Consequences of Non-Compliance

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Withdrawal of system access
- Disciplinary action up to and including dismissal
- Civil or criminal prosecution for serious offences
- Contractual termination for contractors and third parties

All violations are logged, investigated, and reported to HR and the CISO as appropriate.

---

## 10. Exceptions

Requests for exceptions to this policy must be submitted to the CISO with a documented business justification. Exceptions are time-limited, subject to risk assessment, and require written sign-off from the CISO and relevant business owner.
